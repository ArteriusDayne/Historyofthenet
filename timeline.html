Timeline of the Internet

What follows is the History of the Internet

1958

Following 1957 Sputnik launch the Advanced Research Projects Agency is created by the U.S. Defense

1962

The Information Processing Techniques Office which is part of ARPA creates ARPANET to research interactive computing

1972

Electronic mail is introduced by Ray Tomlinson

1973

Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol (TCP/IP) is the standard for computer communication FTP is available to pull files from other computers.

1974

IBM develops SEQUEL (Structured English Query Language )

1976

Jimmy Carter and Walter Mondale plan campaign events during the election year using email Queen Elizabeth is the first state leader to send an email.

1979

Officially 500,000 computers are in use in the US

1982

The word internet is first used

1988

The Internet Worm shuts down 10% of the world’s servers.

1993

50 total World Wide Web servers are available

1994

The White House launches its own website Spamming begins

1995

Java is introduced Amazon.com is founded by Jeff Bezos EBay is founded by Pierre Omidyar Hotmail is started by Jack Smith and Sabeer Bhatia.

1996

There are… 30 Million Users - USA 9 Million Users – Europe 6 Million Users – Asia/Pacific

1997

New traffic records as NASA’s website broadcasts images from Mars with 46 million hits

1998

Google creates their first office in California

2003

CAN-SPAM Act is created to help control the amount of spam they receive Apple Computer creates the Apple iTunes Music Store Average illegal music downloads is 2.6 billion files per month

2004

MyDoom the internet worm spreads through internet servers Online spending reaches $117 billion

2005

YouTube.com is launched

2007

Legal music downloads reach 6.7 million downloads a week World of Warcraft hits 9 Million subscribers worldwide
